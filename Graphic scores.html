Graphic Scores in the Library is a visual score that translates the everyday sounds of the ArtEZ University library into graphic forms. Each shape represents a different acoustic element, such as rhythm, resonance, and intensity. The elements of the score are screen-printed using Puff Ink, allowing viewers to physically touch and feel the texture of the sound. This way, the library’s sounds are experienced not only visually but also tactilely, offering a new way to engage with the space. The final book is physically placed in the ArtEZ library, inviting visitors to explore familiar sounds through both visual and tactile perception. The work demonstrates how sound shapes the atmosphere of a space and how visual representation can expand into a multisensory experience.
