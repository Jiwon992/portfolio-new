Graphic Scores in the Library is a visual score that translates the everyday sounds of the ArtEZ university library into graphic forms. Each graphic shape represents different sound elements such as rhythm, resonance, and intensity. The elements of the score are produced using a silk screen printing technique with puff ink that rises after printing. This allows viewers to touch the book and physically feel the graphic forms of the sounds. Through this, the sounds of the library can be experienced not only visually but also through touch, offering a new way to connect with the space. The final book is placed in the ArtEZ library, where visitors are invited to explore the familiar sounds of the library in a new way through visual and tactile perception. This project shows how sound shapes the atmosphere of a space and how visual representation can expand into a multi sensory experience.
