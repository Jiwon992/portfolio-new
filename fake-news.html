Fake News on Facebook is a publication that collects and organizes fake news data posted on Facebook. All of these data have no factual content and are divided into left, right, and mainstream media based on political orientation. Each post represents a political orientation through the color of the paper, and the information at the bottom of the page shows the source of that data. Some posts have been deleted so that users can no longer check their content, but metadata such as when or where they wrote it remains. In this case, instead of the title of the original post, it is simply marked as "Facebook."
Fake news is often deleted after it has already become widespread. However, the public opinion or perception that has formed as a result does not disappear easily. Therefore, the record of deleted posts is also an important clue to how false information appears, spreads, and then disappears. The book presents the data by changing only the title and color of each post while repeatedly using the same layout, showing how fake news from different political orientations appears within a single structure.
