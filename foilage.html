The foliege photo series explore perception and distortion through the lens of the Surrealist art movement. The work begins with a natural tree branch, which I wrapped entirely in aluminum foil. By doing so, the branch maintains its organic form but gains a reflective, artificial surface. This contrast becomes a key method for questioning how humans interpret, reshape, or even ‘claim’ objects through their own memories and assumptions. The project visualizes the idea that our perception is rarely neutral: when we encounter an object, we often impose familiar patterns such as faces or emotions onto it. By covering a natural object with a man-made material, the work reveals the tension between what is real and what the mind adds on top of it.
