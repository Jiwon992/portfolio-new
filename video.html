Navigation is a video project that documents the process of discovering new spaces by following the movements of other people. In this project, I followed strangers in public spaces and traced their journeys, such as where they took the bus, where they got off, and where they walked. The project was developed over three months. Each video is a one take recording that begins at Arnhem Central Station in the Netherlands and follows one person until they reach their destination. Although every video starts from the same place, the route and destination become completely different depending on who I follow. The project shows that simply following people can become a form of navigation. The narration in the video is based on blog entries that record my thoughts from before I came to the Netherlands, right after I arrived, and now after living here for three years. When I first arrived, I followed people’s actions and words in order to adapt to the unfamiliar environment. Over time, however, I began trying to find my own way instead of simply copying others. Now, after three years, the project reflects how I have gradually come to understand what my own navigation means.
